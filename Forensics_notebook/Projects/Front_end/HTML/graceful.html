Structure matters

Making the structure not overly confusing and navigation simple. This is a key factor to notice and get feedback on as often as you can from actual users. This is an aspect of usability.

Five useful usability test questions:

  • Can users find everything there is to find fairly easily?
  • Can user find specific items if they were asked to?
  • What would a user look at on your site? "What would you look at to decide whether to buy from us?
  • Can users find things they found before, easily? This is a matter of consistency as well.

Another aspect of structure that should not be overlooked is simple navigation tools. This could be a nav bar or an in page search tool such as the one shown in the How TO- Search Bar by W3Schools.

More Info

Accessibility requires designing for conditions. Usabiliy requires desgining for behaviors. Sustainability requires designing for accessibility and usability in two dimensions: time and scale.
